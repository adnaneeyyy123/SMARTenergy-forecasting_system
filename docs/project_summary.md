# Project Summary

## Purpose

Smart Residential Energy Analytics for Dubai Homes helps users understand
synthetic household electricity consumption at room and appliance level. The
project focuses on cooling-heavy residential use, which is especially relevant
to Dubai's hot climate.

## What the project delivers

- Validated, analysis-ready energy records with date and hour features.
- Consumption summaries by room, appliance, day, and hour.
- Explainable anomaly alerts using the IQR method.
- A short-term, linear-trend consumption forecast.
- A Streamlit dashboard for interactive filtering and visual exploration.

## Key findings from the included sample

- Total consumption is **61.57 kWh** across 32 records.
- **Split AC** is the largest appliance category at **36.51 kWh**.
- The largest individual reading is **4.58 kWh**, from a Split AC in the
  H-002 living room at 18:00 on 16 June 2026.

## Practical value

The dashboard helps identify cooling-driven demand, compare rooms and
appliances, investigate unusual readings, and communicate near-term demand.
Its results should guide exploration and energy-saving conversations rather
than automated control or billing decisions.
